# Australia's AI Readiness Checklist

### 10 Critical Areas to Assess Before Your First AI Investment

## How to Use This Checklist

This isn't a quiz. It's a diagnostic tool designed to surface the gaps that derail AI projects — before you've spent money discovering them the hard way.

**For each question:**
- Score yourself honestly (0–2 points)
- Read the "Why it matters" context
- Note any areas where you're uncertain — uncertainty usually signals a gap worth investigating

**Time required:** 15–20 minutes (faster if you have the right people in the room)

**Who should complete this:** Business owners, operations managers, or anyone who would be responsible for implementing or overseeing an AI initiative.

---

## Scoring System

For each question, score yourself:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **0** | We haven't addressed this / No / I don't know |
| **1** | Partially in place / We've started but it's incomplete |
| **2** | Yes, this is documented and current / Fully addressed |

**Maximum possible score: 60 points**

---

## Section 1: Workflow Documentation

### Q1. Can you clearly describe your core business processes in step-by-step detail?

**Score: 0 / 1 / 2**

**Why it matters:** AI doesn't invent workflows — it optimises existing ones. If your team operates on tribal knowledge and "that's how we've always done it," there's nothing concrete for AI to improve. You need documented processes before you can automate them.

*Strong answer looks like:* Key processes are mapped, documented, and accessible. Staff can point to current process docs, not just describe them from memory.

---

### Q2. Do you know which tasks consume disproportionate time relative to their value?

**Score: 0 / 1 / 2**

**Why it matters:** The highest-impact AI use cases target repetitive, time-heavy, low-complexity work. If you can't identify these tasks, you'll either automate the wrong things or struggle to prioritise.

*Strong answer looks like:* You can name 3–5 specific tasks, estimate hours spent weekly, and explain why they're low-value relative to effort.

---

## Section 2: Data Quality & Accessibility

### Q3. Is your business data centralised, clean, and consistently formatted?

**Score: 0 / 1 / 2**

**Why it matters:** AI runs on data. If your data lives in disconnected spreadsheets, inconsistent CRM entries, and someone's email inbox, AI tools will either fail or produce unreliable outputs. "Garbage in, garbage out" isn't a cliché — it's the primary cause of failed AI projects.

*Strong answer looks like:* Core business data (customer records, transactions, inventory, etc.) lives in defined systems with consistent formatting and regular cleaning protocols.

---

### Q4. Can your team access the data they need without IT intervention?

**Score: 0 / 1 / 2**

**Why it matters:** AI tools need data pipelines. If every data request requires a ticket to IT and a two-week wait, your AI implementation will stall repeatedly. Self-serve data access (within appropriate permissions) is a readiness indicator.

*Strong answer looks like:* Key staff can pull reports, export data, and access dashboards independently for their areas of responsibility.

---
